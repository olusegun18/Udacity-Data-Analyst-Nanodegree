Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set represent more than 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. It contains 14 features and 110,527 observations. The dependent variable is the "No-Show" feature and it contains two unique values - NO and YES. NO means the patient did show up for the appointment while YES means the patient didn't show up for the appointment. Further details can be found here

The features found in the dataset are:

  1. PatientId - Unique ID number of the Patient
  2. AppointmentID - Identification of each appointment
  3. Gender - Male or Female
  4. DataMaracaoConsulta - The day of the actual appointment, when they have to visit the doctor
  5. DataAgendamento - The day someone called or registered the appointment, this is before appointment.
  6. Age - How old the Patient is
  7. Neighborhood - Where the appointment takes place
  8. Scholarship - Whether or not the Patient is a benefiaciary of the social welfare program - Bolsa Família. Bolsa Família provided financial aid to poor Brazilian families. You can read more here
  9. Hypertension - Does the Patient have hypertension?
  10. Diabtes - Is the Patient diabetic?
  11. Alcoholism - Is the Patient a lover of alcohol?
  12. Handcap - Is the Patient disabled?
  13. SMS-Recieved - Did the Patient receive any SMS prior to the actual day of the appointment?
  14. No-Show - Did the patient show or not?

Question(s) for Analysis¶

In analyzing this dataset, some questions come to mind. In this analysis, the following questions will be answered:

  1. Is gender an important predictor of whether people will show up or not?
  2. Does age have any say in fixing an appointment with the doctor? Is it also correlated with the "No-Show" feature?
  3. Did patients who received the scholarship show up more than those who didn't?
  4. How did people show up based on the weekday and day_difference?
  5. Did more handicaps show up for their appointments compared to the abled ones?

Conclusion¶

From our analysis, the following were observed:

  1. The values above are really close, which means gender is not a very good predictor of whether people will show up or not
  2. It does seem females generally took their health more seriously
  3. it can be seen that many people from poor backgrounds did show up for their appointments
  4. The analysis above suggests that apart from the children, who maybe are still being taken care of by their parents, people tend to show up as their age increases. The younger ones don't show up the most while the older ones show up more
  5. Patients don't tend to show up on weekends, while Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday has the highest number of turnouts
  6. those with at least an illness turn up more for their appointment

The data that has been analyzed has some limitations. Some of them are:

  • The dataset is small compared to the total population of Brazil.Therefore, it might not be representative of the entire population
  • Many patient_ids are not unique. This means some patients registered more than once and book the same appoitment date on the same scheduled date. This anomaly in the data is why unique patient_ids are retained.
  • The time under which the observations are recorded is too short to make any justifiable and meritable conclusion. All the dates considered are only in 2016 and the highest differemce between scheduled_date and appointment_date was 180days (6months)
  • We created other features to help the data analysis process.
